A Humble Journey Begins...

About Me

Origin

Originally born in Taiwan, I left my homeland country at the age of four and a half for Belize. Travelled with family to several Central America and South America countries. In 2001, my family moved to the United States. The opportunities to live in different third-world countries have allowed me to experience how communities function when faced with limited resources. This interests and experiences have allowed to me explore social theory and now public health.

As a graduating senior at the University of Chicago, I have had the opportunity to delve deeper into my interests in social theory, public health, arts, and basic science research. All of these facets opened a lot of doors for me and I am happy to explore these routes.

Practice

I am a multimedia artist who enjoys working with sculptures, collages, animations, documentary filmmaking, and photography. The ability to play with different media and create something anew is something I love to do. Since 2007, I have continued to work as a freelance photographer and had work with great politicians, individuals, and celebrities. Aside from the art world and my freelancing, I am dabbling in 3D printing and entrepreneurship. Also, I am taking classes at the Starter League (Downtown Chicago) to learn Advanced HTML/CSS and took User Experience (UX) last quarter.

My journey has started and please do check out my journey at either my Art or Design section.

Current Projects

Since November 2011, I have been working independently on my own documentary. The title is Seeing Positively. This documentary explores the process of empowerment among three African Americans underwent a combination of religious oppression, sexual abuse, drugs and gangs, and prostitution.
